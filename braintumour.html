6 Warning Signs of Brain Cancer or Brain Tumour

When you have a severe headache you might worry something's wrong like a brain tumour. However, your odds of developing a cancerous brain tumour are small. Less than 1% symptoms of a brain tumour can have a major impact on your daily life.

However, some brain tumours might not produce any symptoms at all; some major warning signs of a brain tumour include-

1. Balance/Coordination

Your ability to move can be affected by a brain tumour. Tumours especially can lead to clumsy movements or balance problems.

2. Speech Problem

You might develop slurred speech or speech that's hard to understand. At other times you might also have trouble finding the right words to say.

3. Change in Personality

Frontal lobe tumours can cause happy bubbly people to become emotional. If you're normally quiet, these tumours can cause you to become more talkative.

4. Changes in Vision

A tumour affecting the occipital lobe which controls your eyesight might affect your vision, blurred vision, or double vision are also brain tumours sometimes.

5. Seizures

Seizure causes abnormal movements or behaviours. Seizures can cause serious physical injuries, or brain damage.

6. Numbness/Weakness

In the case of a tumour you'll feel these things in one side or part of your body or face. Usually a headache or confusion are your body's way of telling you to hydrate or sleep more.

However ongoing severe headaches especially in the morning can be a brain tumour symptom. Brain tumour headaches tend to persist for more than a few days and may be associated with nausea or vomiting.

If you experience one or several of these brain tumour signs see your doctor. However, changes in your health can also be a symptom of something serious. If you start having unexpected or unexplained health issues, don't wait to get checked out. Early detection of brain tumours are key to a successful treatment plan and just might save your life.
