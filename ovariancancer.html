5 Warning Signs of Ovarian Cancer in Women



Do you women think about your ovaries ? Often these small Walnut shaped organs are a part of your reproductive system. Eggs are created and stored there. The eggs are then released down the Fallopian tubes starting the monthly cycle that ends with either a period or pregnancy.

Most of the time the ovaries do their part on the reproductive team without you having to worry about them. But sometimes Cancer can develop. Even though ovarian cancer isn't that common. It's a deadly condition. Ovarian cancer can be silent. It doesn't always signal to you that it's developing with any signs or signals.

Some people don't even experience symptoms until the cancer has already grown and spread within their body. It's difficult to detect, often causing symptoms that are easy to brush off as everyday aches and pains, but these symptoms can be clues to cancer if you are tuned into your body.

It's important to pay attention to your body if something seems off. Reach out to your doctor. Some of the symptoms may seem like nothing but they can be a warning sign of ovarian cancer.



1. Abdominal Pain

Abdominal pain, having a stomachache or feeling bloated may seem like nothing but if this is outside of your normal, it's good to check out pain in your abdomen and pelvis can be a sign of ovarian cancer.



2. Change in Appetite

Similarly, you might not think of a change in your appetite as something strange; you might run through a list of many reasons why this can happen. However, it can be another sign of ovarian cancer. If your appetite has changed or you notice that you feel full faster. This could be another sign that something isn't right.



3. Abnormal Bleeding

Is there any bleeding or vaginal discharge that happens outside of your typical period? If you notice bleeding at an unusual time, or after menopause, contact your doctor right away.



4. Change in Bowel Habit

Changes to your bowel habits can be another red flag, unusual diarrhoea or constipation can be a sign of developing ovarian cancer. You might also notice that you have to pee more frequently or have urinary urgency and a sudden need to go right away.



5. Lump in Abdomen

Finding a lump in some cases of ovarian cancer. You might feel a lump in your abdomen or notice that your abdomen has suddenly gotten larger. Unfortunately, there are no routine tests to screen for ovarian cancer, but you can understand your risk factors and plan ahead with your doctor.

Some risks to know about include your family history. If people in your family have had ovarian cancer, it's good to let your doctor know. You can receive genetic testing that might help you plan for a possible diagnosis and future he can also be helpful.

Let your doctor know if you were of Eastern European Ashkenazi Jewish background as this can increase your risk of developing ovarian cancer. The chances of ovarian cancer goes up the older you get. If you are pregnant or have endometriosis, your risk of ovarian cancer goes up.

You are also at increased risk if you've had other cancers in the past like breast, uterine or colorectal cancer. Listening to your body and the clues it gives you is really important when it comes to ovarian cancer. When something feels off or outside of your normal. Reach out to your doctor to talk about the symptom.
